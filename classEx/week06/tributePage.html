Louis Armstrong

Music is life itself. What would this world be without good music? No matter what kind it is.

  1. 1923, King Oliver's Creole Jazz Band: "Froggie Moore/Chimes Blues, MandyLee Blues/I'm Going Away to Wear You Off My Mind, Riverside Blues/Mabel's Dream"

  2. 1924-1939, Fletcher Henderson and His Orchestra: "Prince of Wails/Mandy Make Up Your Mind, Hotter Than That/Savoy Blues, I Ain't Got Nobody (And Nobody Cares for Me)/Rockin' Chair, I'm a Ding Dong Daddy from Dumas/I'm in the Market for You, Song of the Islands/ Blue Turning Grey Over You, Elder Eatmore's Sermon on Generosity/Elder Eatmore's Sermon on Throwing Stones, Jeepers Creepers/What Is This Thing Called Swing?"

  3. 1940, Louis Armstrong and Mills Brothers: Marie/Sleepy Time Gal"

  4. 1946-1968: "Endie" / "Do You Know What It Means to Miss New Orleans", "Blueberry Hill" / "That Lucky Old Sun", "La Vie en rose" / "C'est si bon", "(When We Are Dancing) I Get Ideas" / "A Kiss to Build a Dream On", "It Takes Two to Tango" / "I Laughed at Love", "Mack the Knife" / "The Faithful Hussar", "Hello, Dolly" / "A Lot of Livin' To Do", "What a Wonderful World" / "Cabaret", "I Will Wait for You" / "Talk to the Animals"

  5. 1971: Passed away due to a heart attack in his sleep.
